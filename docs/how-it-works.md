# How It Works

Stat Booster quietly upgrades the gear you earn. Every so often, a weapon or
piece of armor you get comes with an **extra bonus stat** on top of whatever it
normally has — for free.

## Where bonuses come from

Your gear can be boosted when you:

- **loot** it (including from dungeon bosses),
- turn in a **quest** for a gear reward,
- **craft** it yourself, or
- win it on a **group roll**.

## What you'll notice

When an item gets boosted you'll see a short message (and hear a small chime),
and the item will show an extra stat on its tooltip — for example a bit more
Strength on a warrior's sword, or extra Intellect on a caster's robe. The bonus
always suits the item, so melee gear gets melee stats and caster gear gets
caster stats.

## How often it happens

It's never guaranteed, and the odds depend on where the item came from. On a
default server:

- **Crafted gear** is boosted most of the time — your effort usually pays off.
- **Looted gear** is boosted a little more often than not, so a second drop is
  very likely to come through.
- **Quest rewards** are boosted now and then, as an occasional pleasant surprise.

Server admins can change these odds (or turn the feature off entirely), so your
server may feel different.

## Not happy with the stat?

There's a special item, the **Attribute Recalibrator**, that lets you re-roll an
item's bonus stat into a new one. Just note that it replaces the current bonus —
so you might end up with something different, for better or worse.

For now the Recalibrator isn't sold by a vendor or dropped by enemies, so you'll
need a server admin to hand one out. A GM can grant it with the command
`.additem 41605` or `.sb additem 41605 1`.
